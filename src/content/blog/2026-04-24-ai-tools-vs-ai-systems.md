"We're already using AI."

I hear this from every corporate client before they onboard with us. And they're right — they are using AI.

They're using it like a very fast Google.

## What "using AI" usually looks like

- Someone Googles a question → now they ask Claude instead
- Someone writes a first draft → now they paste it into ChatGPT to clean it up
- Someone makes a slide deck → now they use Gemini to generate bullet points

None of this is wrong. It's genuinely useful.

But it's also not leverage. It's productivity, not transformation.

## The leverage gap

Here's what the companies pulling ahead are doing differently:

They're not asking "what can AI do for me today?" They're asking "what process can I redesign so AI makes it 10x faster every time?"

That's the leverage gap. And it compounds.

A team that saves 30 minutes a day per person through better prompting gains 130 hours per year per employee.

A team that redesigns their proposal workflow to use AI saves 4 hours **per proposal** — and the output is better.

## Why tools alone don't close the gap

AI tools (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity — pick your stack) are getting more capable every month. But capability without process is noise.

If your team doesn't have:
- A shared library of tested prompts
- Standard operating procedures that include AI steps
- Someone accountable for iterating on what works

...then every upgrade to the underlying model is just more potential that goes untapped.

## Where to start

You don't need a new software subscription. You need a workflow audit.

Pick one repetitive task your team does weekly. Map it out. Find where AI can do the heavy lifting. Document what works.

That one workflow — documented, tested, repeatable — is worth more than 50 one-off prompts.

That's what we help build in our workshops. Start with one. Build from there.
